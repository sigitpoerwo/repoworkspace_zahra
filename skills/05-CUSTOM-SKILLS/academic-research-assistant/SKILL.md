## Workflow

### Literature Review Process

1. **Define Research Question**
   - Clarify research topic
   - Identify key concepts
   - Define scope and boundaries

2. **Search Strategy**
   - Develop search keywords
   - Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT)
   - Filter by year, journal quality
   - Snowball from key papers

3. **Paper Selection**
   - Inclusion/exclusion criteria
   - Quality assessment
   - Relevance screening

4. **Analysis & Synthesis**
   - Extract key findings
   - Identify themes and patterns
   - Map research landscape
   - Find research gaps

5. **Documentation**
   - Citation management
   - Summary tables
   - Concept matrices
   - Reference list

### Research Paper Writing

1. **Title & Abstract**
   - Clear, concise title
   - Structured abstract (background, methods, results, conclusion)

2. **Introduction**
   - Research background
   - Problem statement
   - Research objectives
   - Significance of study

3. **Literature Review**
   - Theoretical framework
   - Previous studies
   - Research gap identification
   - Hypothesis development

4. **Methodology**
   - Research design
   - Population and sample
   - Data collection methods
   - Analysis techniques

5. **Results**
   - Descriptive statistics
   - Hypothesis testing
   - Key findings presentation

6. **Discussion**
   - Interpretation of results
   - Comparison with previous studies
   - Theoretical implications
   - Practical implications

7. **Conclusion**
   - Summary of findings
   - Limitations
   - Future research directions

## Citation Formats

### APA 7th Edition
```
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, Volume(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxx
```

### IEEE
```
[1] A. A. Author and B. B. Author, "Title of article," Journal Name, vol. X, no. Y, pp. XX-YY, Month Year.
```

### Harvard
```
Author, A.A. and Author, B.B. (Year) 'Title of article', Journal Name, Volume(Issue), pp. XX-YY.
```
